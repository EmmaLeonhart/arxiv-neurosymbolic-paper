Dot Product Visualizer

The dot product measures how much two vectors point in the same direction. Drag the vector tips to see it change in real time.

How it works

The dot product of two vectors A and B equals:

A · B = |A| × |B| × cos(θ)

where θ is the angle between them. This gives you three intuitive cases:

  • Same direction (θ < 90°) → positive dot product
  • Perpendicular (θ = 90°) → dot product is exactly zero
  • Opposite directions (θ > 90°) → negative dot product

The projection (the thick translucent line) shows how much of A lies along B's direction. Try dragging A perpendicular to B and watch the projection vanish.
